How it works

Each month, the strategy computes the realized downside volatility of SPY over the prior 21 trading days. It then sizes the monthly buy to bring total exposure toward a 20% annualized downside volatility target.

When markets are calm, the full monthly allocation is deployed. When realized volatility exceeds the target, the strategy scales back — or holds — and parks idle capital in a dólar-linked money market fund (Balanz Fondo Dólar Linked). No selling unless a sustained volatility breach triggers a REDUCE alert.

Signal logic:

  • Instrument: SPY (CEDEAR, 10:1 ratio, Balanz)
  • Volatility measure: downside semi-deviation of daily returns
  • Lookback: 21 trading days
  • Vol target: 20% annualized
  • Leverage: none — strategy can only de-risk, never lever up
  • Smoothing: EMA(10) + 10% no-trade band to reduce unnecessary turnover
  • Cadence: monthly execution, first trading day of the month
  • Idle capital: Balanz Fondo Dólar Linked

Why CEDEARs

CEDEARs (Certificados de Depósito Argentinos) are Argentine depositary receipts that track USD-denominated underlying assets like SPY. They trade on BYMA in pesos but are priced at the MEP dollar rate, giving Argentine investors effective USD exposure without needing to move money offshore.

SPY Core operates entirely on local infrastructure — no foreign account, no capital transfer, no AFIP complications. You buy CEDEARs on Balanz, the system tells you how many, you execute.
